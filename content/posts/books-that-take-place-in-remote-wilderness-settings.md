If you are a nature enthusiast or simply enjoy stories set in remote wilderness settings, then you are in for a treat! Books that take place in the great outdoors offer readers a chance to immerse themselves in the beauty and challenges of nature, providing a sense of adventure and escapism. In this blog post, we have curated a list of five captivating books that will transport you to remote wilderness settings and take you on thrilling journeys filled with exploration, survival, and discovery.

---

### 1. "Into the Wild" by Jon Krakauer

"Into the Wild" is a non-fiction book that tells the gripping and tragic story of Christopher McCandless, a young man who ventured into the Alaskan wilderness in search of adventure and self-discovery. Krakauer's narrative explores McCandless's journey, detailing his experiences, encounters with nature, and his ultimate fate. This book offers a poignant reflection on the allure and dangers of the wilderness, as well as the complexities of human nature.

### 2. "Wild: From Lost to Found on the Pacific Crest Trail" by Cheryl Strayed

In "Wild," Cheryl Strayed recounts her solo hike along the Pacific Crest Trail, a challenging and remote wilderness route that spans from California to Washington. Through her introspective and raw storytelling, Strayed shares her personal struggles, triumphs, and encounters with the natural world. This memoir is a testament to the transformative power of nature and the resilience of the human spirit.

### 3. "The Call of the Wild" by Jack London

A classic tale of adventure and survival, "The Call of the Wild" follows the journey of Buck, a domesticated dog who is thrust into the harsh and unforgiving wilderness of the Yukon during the Klondike Gold Rush. Jack London's vivid prose captures the beauty and brutality of the natural world, as Buck undergoes a profound transformation in order to survive and thrive in his new environment.

### 4. "The River" by Peter Heller

"The River" is a riveting novel that follows two friends, Wynn and Jack, on a canoeing trip in the remote wilderness of Northern Canada. When they hear a couple arguing on the riverbank, they become entangled in a series of events that test their survival skills and their friendship. Peter Heller's atmospheric writing and suspenseful plot make this book a gripping read that will keep you on the edge of your seat.

### 5. "Where the Crawdads Sing" by Delia Owens

Set in the marshes of North Carolina, "Where the Crawdads Sing" is a mesmerizing novel that weaves together the story of Kya Clark, a young woman who grows up in isolation in the wilderness. As Kya navigates the natural world around her and grapples with the complexities of human relationships, secrets from her past come to light, leading to a stunning revelation. Delia Owens' lyrical prose and vivid descriptions of the marsh make this book a captivating and immersive read.

---

These books that take place in remote wilderness settings offer readers a chance to explore the untamed beauty of nature, the resilience of the human spirit, and the transformative power of adventure. Whether you are drawn to tales of survival, self-discovery, or the mysteries of the natural world, these captivating stories will transport you to remote and wild landscapes, where thrilling adventures and poignant reflections await. So grab a book, pack your bags (or stay cozy at home), and embark on a literary journey into the heart of the wilderness. Happy reading!
