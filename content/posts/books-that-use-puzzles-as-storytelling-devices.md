Books that incorporate puzzles as storytelling devices offer readers a unique and engaging reading experience. These stories often challenge readers to think critically, solve complex mysteries, and unravel intricate plotlines. If you enjoy books that keep you on the edge of your seat and test your wits, here are five captivating reads that use puzzles as key elements in their narratives:

### 1. "The Da Vinci Code" by Dan Brown

"The Da Vinci Code" is a gripping thriller that follows symbologist Robert Langdon as he unravels a series of cryptic clues to solve a murder mystery that leads to a hidden secret with profound implications. Brown weaves together elements of art, history, and religion to create a fast-paced and suspenseful story that keeps readers guessing until the very end. As Langdon deciphers intricate puzzles and uncovers hidden messages, readers are drawn into a world of mystery and intrigue that challenges their deductive skills.

### 2. "Mr. Penumbra's 24-Hour Bookstore" by Robin Sloan

In "Mr. Penumbra's 24-Hour Bookstore," Sloan blends elements of mystery, technology, and literature to craft a story that revolves around a secret society, a mysterious bookstore, and a quest for immortality. The protagonist, Clay Jannon, embarks on a journey to uncover the truth behind the bookstore's enigmatic customers and the ancient texts they seek to decode. As Clay delves deeper into the bookstore's secrets, he must solve puzzles that span centuries and unlock the key to a hidden knowledge that could change the course of history.

### 3. "The Westing Game" by Ellen Raskin

"The Westing Game" is a classic mystery novel that follows a group of sixteen heirs who are invited to participate in a game to solve the murder of eccentric millionaire Samuel W. Westing. The heirs must unravel a series of cryptic clues, puzzles, and word games to uncover the identity of the murderer and claim their share of Westing's fortune. Raskin's intricate plot and clever wordplay keep readers guessing as they try to piece together the puzzle of Westing's death and the hidden connections between the heirs.

### 4. "The 7½ Deaths of Evelyn Hardcastle" by Stuart Turton

"The 7½ Deaths of Evelyn Hardcastle" is a mind-bending mystery that combines elements of time travel, suspense, and psychological intrigue. The protagonist, Aiden Bishop, wakes up each day in a different host body at Blackheath Manor, tasked with solving the murder of Evelyn Hardcastle to break a cycle of eternal repetition. As Aiden relives the same day from multiple perspectives, he must navigate a complex web of clues, puzzles, and deceit to uncover the truth behind Evelyn's death and escape the time loop.

### 5. "S." by J.J. Abrams and Doug Dorst

"S." is a unique literary experience that blurs the line between fiction and reality through its innovative storytelling format. The book is presented as a novel called "Ship of Theseus" by a mysterious author, V.M. Straka, with handwritten notes, annotations, and ephemera exchanged between two readers in the margins. As the readers, Jen and Eric, decode the hidden messages, puzzles, and conspiracies within "Ship of Theseus," they become embroiled in a real-life mystery that parallels the novel's narrative. "S." offers a multi-layered and immersive reading experience that challenges readers to solve puzzles, decipher codes, and unravel the interconnected stories within the book.

These books showcase the creative ways in which puzzles can be integrated into storytelling, providing readers with an interactive and intellectually stimulating reading experience. Whether you enjoy unraveling cryptic clues, deciphering hidden messages, or piecing together intricate plotlines, these captivating reads are sure to keep you engaged from start to finish. Get ready to embark on a thrilling journey of mystery, intrigue, and discovery with these puzzle-centric books!
